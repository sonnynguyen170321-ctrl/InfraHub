Why Companies Struggle

Most organizations don't have a technology problem. They have a coordination problem. Here's what that looks like.

01

Too Many Vendors

Each layer of the stack comes from a different provider. Multiple contracts, multiple SLAs, multiple support teams, and no one owns the whole picture.

02

Complex Procurement

RFPs, vendor evaluation, quote negotiation, approval chains. Procurement cycles measured in months, not days. By the time you sign, the requirements have already changed.

03

Long Deployment

Between lead times, cross-connect provisioning, and configuration, standing up new infrastructure takes 90-180 days. The business moves faster than the network.

04

Fragmented Support

When something breaks, fingers point between vendors. Diagnostics happen across disconnected dashboards. Resolution takes hours because no one sees the full stack.

05

Growing Cyber Risks

Infrastructure deployed without security-first architecture creates attack surfaces that compound with every expansion. Each new connection is a new vulnerability.
